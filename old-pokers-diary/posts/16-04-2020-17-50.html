I haven't been here for a while, so I'll keep it short with more recent news.

I've got a couple new speedgames. Hotline Miami is pretty exciting, as it requires a lot of skill and a pretty good warm up every time we touch I get this feeling and everytime we kiss I swear I can fly. I've also tried running a game I vaguely remembered from my childhood - Sammy Suricate in Lion Land. Turned out it made me and some other people from the Polish community actively look for skips in it for about three days. We've managed to find skips (as in, "ignore the level, go to the end" skips) for almost all of the 36 levels in the game. I kept finding new ones afterwards and now the game is REALLY broken. I also wanted some simple short JRPG or something similar to run while watching something or during e-classes. Turned out Pokemon Blue with a route from 2015 (so no manips) was pretty much perfect. Got a 19:08 and wanted to stop there, submit it and move to probably learning manips, but I didn't record a hard reset, so my PB is invalid, which puts me in a pretty weird state. No idea if I should go for sub 19 or learn manips, we'll see, I guess, not that it's the only game I do recently.

As of recent speedrunning events, ESA Winter was awesome as always. GSPS Dzieciom 2020 raised a record-breaking 10111 PLN (about $2410) and turned out to be amazing as well. Already writing down ideas for GSPS 2020.
